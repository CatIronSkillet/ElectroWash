Nature Films

Still echoing the sounds of electronica, but guiding our gaze to images of nature

The idea is to be ready to capture interesting things in nature especially when they happen right in front of you. The background music features essentially the same electronic instruments featured throughout Electro Wash

Here I was strolling up to the top of Twin Peaks, San Francisco on a most glorious afternoon. When the skies are clear, and they often aren't, the view of the Pacific Ocean is most welcome. And it's my belief that this pleasant sight is not lost on our avian friends.

On a rare day trip through Alameda, I came across a terribly photogenic gaggle. I reassured them to not mind me, that I was not there to compete for whatever precious resources this park had to offer. I appreciated their hospitality and told them they would be most welcome in San Francisco.

Here another of our avian friends was taking a dip in the San Francisco bay, which is rather cold year round, but I assume this fellow runs warm.

Sometimes it's wise to just have a gander out the window. On this occasion I noticed three more of our winged friends huddled together on a street light. Luckily enough I began recording in slow motion. I thought perhaps they would take off all at once but they were nice enough to go one at a time.
